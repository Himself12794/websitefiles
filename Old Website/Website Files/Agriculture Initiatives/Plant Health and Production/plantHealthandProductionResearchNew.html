Shirley L. Chao
(Biological Science)

The research of Dr. Shirley Chao of the Department of Biological Sciences focuses on agricultural pesticides and toxicity. She has recently developed a patent pending "green" pesticide that is useful on the storage of grains.

Abdelmajid Kassem
(Biological Science)

Dr. Abdelmajid Kassem is the Chair of the Department of Biological Sciences. His research focuses on plant genetics and genomics and genetic and QTL mapping of important agronomic traits in major crops.

Daryush Ila
(Research Office)

Dr. Daryush ILA, the Associate Vice Chancellor for Research at Fayetteville State University, holds an M.S. in Nuclear Physics and a PhD in Condensed Matter Physics. He has recently focused his research in the area of producing agricultural crops in the desert, through a trade secret method referred to as "tiles".

Lieceng Zhu
(Biological Science)

Impact of environmental stress on the resistance of plant-parasite interaction
The goal of my research is to investigate, at molecular level, the role of environmental stress in responses of host plants to parasitic attacks. We use wheat-Hessian fly as a model system to determine the impacts of heat stress on metabolism of wheat plants infested by Hessian fly. The findings from our research will enhance our understanding of the mechanism of loss of resistance in wheat to Hessian fly and the vulnerability of plant resistance to parasite in the context of environmental stress and climate change.

Carla Padilla
(Research Office)

Dr. Carla Padilla of the Research Office is currently investigating cost-effective, reduced-water methods to grow agricultural crops in the desert, using locally occurring minerals and topography.
