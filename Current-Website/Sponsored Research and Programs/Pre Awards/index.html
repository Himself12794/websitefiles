Procedure When there is No Limit on the Number of Proposal Submissions per Institution

Steps for the submission process:

  1. Notify OSRP at sdudley@uncfsu.edu of your intent to submit as soon as you know you plan to apply, or at least 30 days prior to the due date.
  2. Contact our budget officer, as soon as possible (at least 10 working days prior proposal deadline). He is available to assist you with your budget preparation, once a draft budget, budget narrative and proposal are provided.
  3. Contact our pre-awards administrator for RAMSeS, NSF Fastlane or Grants.gov training if needed.
  4. If you are requesting release time, email the Request for Release Time form to your chair and dean for approval and attach the form in RAMSeS.
  5. Submit in RAMSeS your full proposal, budget, budget narrative and all supporting documents to OSRP no later than 7 working days prior to the due date to allow ample time to review your proposal for corrections and assistance.
  6. Submit and certify your final proposal and final grants.gov application package in RAMSeS (at least 5 working days prior to due date).
  7. Email OSRP, your chair and dean that your proposal is complete and now ready for approval in RAMSeS.
  8. Please plan to submit your proposal to the agency at least 2 days prior to the due date to allow for technical difficulties or to allow time to fix errors or warnings found during the submission process.

Please note: If your proposal will require the need for a new or an upgraded/modified campus facility, please contact Mr. Rudolph Cardenas, Associate Vice Chancellor for Facilities at rcardena@uncfsu.edu for prior approval.

If your proposal will require IT support, IT personnel, installation, computer equipment or software needs, please contact Mr. Nick Ganesan, Associate Vice Chancellor for Information Technology at nganesan@uncfsu.edu for prior approval; and If your proposal will require Faculty Development services, training and/or support, please contact Dr. Bonnie Grohe, Director, Office of Faculty Development at bgrohe@uncfsu.edu for prior approval.

Please note that all proposals that are submitted to the Agency without prior University approval may be withdrawn if all approvals are not received.

Thank you and please feel free to contact our office at 910-672-1570 if you have any questions or need any assistance.


Procedure When there is a Limit on the Number of Proposal Submissions per Institution

When there is a limit on the number of proposals that may be submitted for a solicitation, to ensure that the university submits the most competitive proposal, an internal review and selection process by will be implemented.

Steps for the selection process:
  1. Notify OSRP at sdudley@uncfsu.edu of your intent to submit as soon as you know you plan to apply, or at least 6 weeks prior to the due date.
  2. Contact our budget officer, as soon as possible. He is available to assist you with your budget preparation, once a draft budget, budget narrative and proposal are provided
  3. Submit in RAMSeS a Draft of your proposal, budget and all required documents a minimum of 30 days prior to the agency due date.
  4. Your draft proposal will be submitted to administration for review and selection.
  5. You will be notified by OSRP if your proposal has been selected or not within 7 days of receipt.
  6. If your proposal has been selected, please submit your Final proposal, budget and all required documents in the requested agency format, a minimum of 14 days prior to the agency due date.
Review Criteria:

Reviewers will be asked to evaluate all proposals against two criteria:

Intellectual Merit: The Intellectual Merit criterion encompasses the potential to advance knowledge

Broader Impacts: The Broader Impacts criterion encompasses the potential to benefit society and contribute to the achievement of specific, desired societal outcomes.

     The following elements should be considered in the review for both criteria:

  1. What is the potential for the proposed activity to:
    1. Advance knowledge and understanding within its own field or across different fields (Intellectual Merit); and
    2. Benefit society or advance desired societal outcomes (Broader Impacts)?
  2. To what extent do the proposed activities suggest and explore creative, original, or potentially transformative Concepts?
      3. Is the plan for carrying out the proposed activities well- reasoned, well-organized, and based on a sound rationale? Does the plan incorporate a mechanism to assess success?
    1. How well qualified is the individual, team, or organization to conduct the proposed activities?
    2. Are there adequate resources available to the PI (either at the home organization or through collaborations) to carry out the proposed activities?
